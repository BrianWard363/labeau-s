History

1981

Renee LaBeau and her husband Robert had always wanted to own a business. In early 1981, they saw an ad for a drive-in up for sale in Garden City, Utah. Shortly after purchasing it, they changed the name from Western Drive-In to the well-known name La Beau's. Although the business started slow, it gradually grew to become the most popular place at Bear Lake.

1982

A year after LaBeau's opened, a family loss almost led Renee to shut down the restaurant as her husband Robert had passed away. However, despite the advice from many to sell the restaurant, she decided not to make any major decisions in the first year after his passing, following the advice of a church member.

1994

As business contined to grow we needed more space to open to our customers so we decided to open another location right across the street from La Beau's called La Beau's Too.

2017-2018

Mrs. LaBeau, with a heart full of gratitude, kept her business in great hands and wanted to keep it family-owned and family-run. She decided to sell it to her grandson, Jeff Allen, who continues to provide great-tasting shakes and food for tourists from all parts of the earth.
